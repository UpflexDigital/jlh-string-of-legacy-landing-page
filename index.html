Four distinct expressions inspired by the iconic Gibson guitars that shaped John Lee Hooker's legendary sound. Each bourbon tells a chapter of the story.

At John Lee Hooker Legacy Spirits, we believe a life's work can be tasted as well as heard. My grandfather, John Lee Hooker, poured his soul into his music, and his iconic Gibson guitars were essential to his powerful sound.

We are proud to partner with Gibson for the Strings of Legacy Kentucky Bourbon Collection. This limited edition series features four distinct 8 year old, cask strength single barrels distilled at Bardstown Bourbon Company.

"This is history you can taste."

Chapter One - 1940's

Epiphone
Zephyr

This single barrel takes us back to our roots, paying tribute to the Epiphone Zephyr, a guitar that likely echoed through the earliest days of Granddad's incredible journey.

This bourbon pays homage to those first days, the raw talent and the blood, sweat and tears that launched it all.

8.5
Proof
122.8
Mash
78/10/12

Tasting Notes

Nose: Sugar cookie, vanilla icing, warm graham cracker
Palate: Sweet stone fruit, apricot, peach, cinnamon
Finish: Clean, satisfying, quick finish

– Glenn Thomas, Founder

Chapter Two - 1950's

Gibson
SJ-200

This single barrel release hits close to home for me, a tribute to Granddad's magnificent Gibson SJ-200. This was the guitar I remembered most clearly from growing up, the one he'd often play in the house.

Its deep, resonant voice filled our home with the blues, just for me. It wasn't about the stage then; it was about family, about giving his gift.

Age
8.5
Proof
125.3
Mash
78/10/12

Tasting Notes

Nose: Espresso bean, dark chocolate, charred oak
Palate: Chocolate dipped black cherry, rye spice, molasses
Finish: Cracked pepper, rich soil, cured tobacco

– Glenn Thomas, Founder

Chapter Three - 1960's

Les Paul
Goldtop

I think the Les Paul Goldtop was always just a different energy for Granddad. It was an edgier, louder sound and this single barrel captures that same fierce vibe.

It recalls those periods when he'd really cut loose onstage, that raw intensity that was just so riveting.

Age
8.5
Proof
122
Mash
78/10/12

Tasting Notes

Nose: Soft vanilla bean, sweet cream, wildflowers
Palate: Creamy, buttery texture, black pepper, honey
Finish: Long, warm finish of seasoned oak and old leather

– Glenn Thomas, Founder
